Bugatti Chiron Super Sport
2022 - date		  

Profile

Think of the new Super Sport as a more civilised, road friendly version of the bonkers 300+.

The 1600hp quad-turbo W16 is retained, but top speed has been limited to a mere 273mph. Due to the increased power, the Super Sport shaves 1.5 seconds from the standard Chiron's 0-300kmh sprint.

For high speed stability, the Super Sport shares the same elongated bodywork of the 300+, with two sets of vertically stacked exhausts protruding from the rear. There's also the signature Bugatti 'pepperpot' cooling holes atop both front wings. Thankfully, the interior trim is still the leather lined luxurious environment you'd expect from a Bugatti.

As is usual in the world of the ultra-wealthy Bugatti collector, the run of 30 Super Sports are all already sold out.
